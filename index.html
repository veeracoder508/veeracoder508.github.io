The Green Heart of Chennai: A Nature Guide to Adyar Eco Park
Tholkappia Poonga

Welcome to the Adyar Eco Park, a restored slice of the historic Coromandel Coast right in Chennai. This is a guide to the fragile estuary ecosystem, where river water meets the Bay of Bengal, nurturing unique mangroves and migratory birds. Discover how the city's lungs were brought back to life, acting as a crucial defense against floods and a centre for environmental education.

Where the River Meets the Sea
The Crucial Brackish Zone

The Adyar Creek is a narrow backwater of the Adyar River before it meets the Bay of Bengal, forming a crucial estuary. This area is defined by its mix of freshwater and saltwater, creating a unique brackish environment that supports life specially adapted to constant salinity changes.

Biodiversity
Life in the Saltwater

The park's biodiversity is the result of focused conservation efforts, planting over 170 species of endemic flora that belong to the Tropical Dry Evergreen Forest ecosystem of the Coromandel coast.

Species Type Example Species Special Feature
Mangroves Rhizophora spp. (Indian Mangrove) Pneumatophores (aerial roots) that help the plant breathe in waterlogged, salty soil.
Coastal Forest Neem, Palmyra Palm Stabilize sand dunes and provide essential shade and habitat.
Wetland Plants Reeds and Sedges Natural filters, crucial for improving water quality in the creek.

Value Beyond Beauty
The Eco Park's Impact on the Community

The park's benefit to Chennai extends beyond its scenic beauty, providing vital social and economic services.

Aspect Impact on Chennai & R A Puram
Environmental Education Serves as a major Education Centre for schools, offering guided tours to teach students about mangroves and coastal ecology.
Climate Regulation The vast green cover and water body help mitigate the urban heat island effect, offering a cooler microclimate to the surrounding residential areas
Property Value The presence of a protected natural area significantly enhances the quality of life and the economic value of real estate in the R A Puram vicinity.
Water Security The park's restored wetlands play a key, indispensable role in groundwater recharge for the city.

Protecting the Green Heart
Visit Responsibly

The Adyar Eco Park is a symbol of successful restoration and must be treated with respect. Its fragile ecosystem requires minimal human impact to thrive.


Visitor Guidelines:
Timing is Key: Visit early morning (6:00 AM - 9:00 AM) for the best chance to spot diverse birdlife.

No Littering: Use designated bins only. Do not introduce any plastic or foreign material into the water or plants.

Photography: Simple, non-commercial photography is permitted. Avoid using flash near wildlife, especially birds.

The Big Threat: Pollution remains a continuous challenge. Support efforts to monitor and stop sewage discharge into the Adyar River upstream.
